Gravitational wave astrophysicist

I grew up in the Capital Region of New York. My family lived in Scotia, just outside of Schenectady, and I graduated from Mohonasen High School in 2005. I earned bachelor's degrees in physics and journalism from Syracuse University in 2009, where I first began work on exciting gravitational wave research with the LIGO detectors. I graduated with a Master's a PhD in physics in 2015 from the University of Massachusetts Amherst, under the mentorship of Laura Cadonati.

I'm currently a postdoctoral scholar in experimental physics with the LIGO Laboratory at Caltech, in Pasadena, California. I am one of the leaders of the LIGO Detector Characterization group in the international, award-winning LIGO Scientific Collaboration. I was based at the LIGO-Livingston site at the time of the GW150914 discovery, which was a wonderful opportunity.

I am passionate about promoting diversity in the STEAM fields.
